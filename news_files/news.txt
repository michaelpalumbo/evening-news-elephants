Paleontologists worldwide are abuzz after a perfectly preserved bison carcass was discovered in a bog in Wood Buffalo National Park (AB). Preliminary DNA analysis has dated the animal as almost 12,000 years old, making it one of the oldest recorded specimens of wild bison in the world. Moreover, the low oxygen conditions allow for complex testing from the creatures hair, which might offer new insight into how the creatures that once covered the prairies might have lived.
